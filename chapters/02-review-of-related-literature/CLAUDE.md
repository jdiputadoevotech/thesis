# Chapter 2 (Review of Related Literature) — Grading Criteria

Reference this when outlining or modifying `draft.md` in this folder. These are the professor's requirements (source: `professor-criteria.md`).

## Purpose (what a passing RRL does)
Critically **synthesize** existing studies to establish the academic foundation of the study by showing:
- **What is known** — theories/concepts that frame the research + findings from past studies.
- **What is missing** — gaps, contradictions, underexplored areas.
- **Why the study matters** — position the research in the broader scholarly conversation.

## Non-negotiable rules
1. **Synthesize, don't list.** Never do a study-by-study summary. Group sources by theme; combine multiple sources per sentence to show convergence.
2. **Show agreement AND contrast.** Cluster sources that agree (e.g. combined into one citation group) and explicitly surface sources that disagree or caution.
3. **End every thematic block with a research gap.** After agreement + contrast, state what is missing that the thesis addresses.
4. **APA in-text citations** — `(Author, Year)` or narrative `Author (Year)`; multiple sources in one parenthetical separated by `;` and alphabetized.
5. **APA references list** — alphabetized by first author's surname, italicized journal/book titles, DOIs/URLs when available, hanging indent.

## Paragraph template (apply per theme)
1. **Introduce the theme** — general statement about the sub-topic being reviewed.
2. **Agreement (grouped)** — 2+ studies supporting the same idea in one integrative sentence.
3. **Specific examples** — detail from 1–2 of those studies to illustrate.
4. **Contrast** — study/studies that highlight limitations, risks, or opposing findings.
5. **Synthesize + gap** — bring agreement and contrast together, then name the gap.

## Transition words to use
- **Agreement:** Similarly, In addition, Likewise, Consistently, "Several studies agree that…", "This is supported by…".
- **Contrast:** However, In contrast, On the other hand, Conversely, "While some studies suggest…, others argue…", "Despite these findings…".
- **Synthesis → gap:** "Taken together…", "These findings collectively suggest…", "The literature reveals both strengths and limitations…", "This tension highlights the need for…", "Few studies have examined…", "This gap underscores…".

## Self-check before considering a section done
- [ ] No study-by-study listing — sources are integrated.
- [ ] Both agreement and contrast are shown.
- [ ] Each theme ends with an explicit gap.
- [ ] All in-text citations are valid APA and exist in `literature/references.md`.
- [ ] References are alphabetized, italicized titles, hanging indent, DOIs/URLs present.
