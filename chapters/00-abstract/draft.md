# Abstract

<!-- ~250 words, single paragraph. Draft after Ch1 §1.1 lands. See notes.md. -->

Generative AI image tools such as Gemini, GPT Image, and Midjourney now produce finished graphics with rendered text, yet they render that text through probabilistic glyph deformation (micro-warping of strokes, distorted spacing, and merged or invented letterforms), a failure this study terms typographic hallucination. When a designer wants to reuse such typography, the first step is to name the font, and it is exactly this step that current tools break on. Established font recognition assumes a geometrically intact glyph, so classifiers trained on pristine specimens overfit and collapse in the wild; commercial identifiers add a further cost, mapping shapes to paid licenses, and none can abstain when a glyph belongs to no catalogued face. This study proposes an open-set metric-learning framework that maps a hallucinated text crop to a ranked shortlist of the closest fonts in a localized palette of the top 50 to 100 open-source Google Fonts, or to an "unknown" verdict when no palette font applies, enabling free, license-clear typographic reconstruction. A synthetic data pipeline renders clean word crops and degrades them with controlled operators (elastic warp, blur and noise, and kerning jitter) to reproduce documented generative deformation. A metric embedding built on a frozen self-supervised Vision Transformer separates typographic identity from deformation, and a post-hoc rejection score handles the open-set case. Evaluation combines Top-K accuracy, a re-render-and-compare structural-similarity distance, a confusion matrix organized by typographic family, and a three-person human-proxy panel on real generative crops. The framework is expected to contribute a deformation-robust, license-clear approach to open-set font recognition; empirical results are deferred until training and evaluation runs complete.

**Keywords:** typographic hallucination; font style recognition; metric learning; open-set recognition; generative AI
