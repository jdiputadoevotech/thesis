## Presentation flow (chair-led opening)
1. Chair greets + acknowledges proponents.
2. Chair introduces panel members.
3. Proponents introduce themselves.
4. Proponents lead a short prayer.
5. Chair states flow (30 / 40 / 20 above) + rubric.
6. Chair opens floor for questions/clarifications before start.

## Slide structure (target ~11–20 slides)

### 1. Big Picture / Context — 1–2 slides
- Establish the territory (image or text).
- 1–2 sentences: why this topic matters now (industry / society / field).
- = **Rationale of the Study**.

### 2. Specific Problem (The Conflict) — 2–3 slides
- Narrow to exact pain point: what breaks, costs money, or wastes effort.
- Back with a concrete statistic or citation (prove it's real).
- Cover: **Statement of the Problem**, **Significance of the Study**, **Scope & Limitation**.

### 3. The Gap (What We Don't Know) — 2–3 slides
- Most critical part of the rationale.
- What current literature/solutions miss — the blind spot this study targets.
- Highlight most relevant RRL studies; name the gap in each.

### 4. Your Solution (The Value Add) — 6–12 slides
- State how the thesis fills that exact gap; justify doing the study.
- Cover:
  - Research Environment & Respondents
  - Research Instrument / Sources of Data
  - Research Procedure
  - Conceptual Framework
  - Analysis & Design
  - Development Model
  - Development Approaches
  - Software Development Tools
  - Project Management — Schedule & Timeline, Responsibilities, Budget & Cost
  - Verification, Validation & Testing (VV&T)
  - Software Requirements Specification (SRS)

## The Gold Standard Rule
Panel judges **coachability**. A flawed proposal presented by a student who takes
feedback gratefully and intelligently beats a perfect proposal presented
defensively. Accept critique graciously.
